MENTAL HEALTH GUIDE

The part of Mental wellness that covers our social, psychological, and emotional well-being is called mental health. A type of well-being known as mental health allows us to manage life's obstacles, reach our full potential, study and work effectively, and give back to our community.

Many healthy habits and behaviors are formed during teenage years, which is a special and creative period that lasts into adulthood. Also, significant changes in growth occur during puberty. Teenagers who face physical, emotional, and social obstacles, such as poverty or violence, may be more open to mental health issues.

>

Mental health includes emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It is more than the absence of a mental illness—it’s essential to your overall health and quality of life. Self-care can play a role in maintaining your mental health and help support your treatment and recovery if you have a mental illness.
